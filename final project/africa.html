Africa

All coffee plants are the progeny of seedlings that were taken from Africa--Ethiopia to be precise. Coffee varietals continue to proliferate in Ethiopia, both cultivars and over 1,000 wild heirloom varietals. Not all of these are suited to coffee production, but the best coffees in Ethiopia and the rest of East Africa share sparkling sweet acidic notes, ranging from tropical fruits to delicate florals.

Exemplars

Ethiopia: Naturally processed Ethiopian coffees tend to be more jammy, with their rich sweet berry flavor and a heavy, luscious body. Washed Ethiopian coffees skew cleaner and floral, with a jasmine or lemongrass tea-like delicacy, a lighter mouthfeel, and a drier finish.

Kenya: Kenyan coffees a mouth-puckering savory-sweet characteristic that sometimes manifests as a tomato-like acidity, other times a black-currant tartness. Good-quality Kenyan coffees tend to evoke the tropics with their sparkling fruit notes..

Rwanda: The fruit notes in Rwandan coffees tend to be more subdued, but what they may lack in flamboyant fruit they make up with a rich mouthfeel, and smooth silky butterscotch.

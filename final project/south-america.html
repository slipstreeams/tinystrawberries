South America

South American coffees range from the typical Colombian profile ~ even, well-rounded taste with mellow acidity and caramel sweetness ~ to the heavier Brazilian flavor profile, which can have a delicious rich peanutty note that oozes over chocolatey undertones. Undestandably, many espresso blends include a Brazilian component.

Coffee Growing Profile

Growing Altitude

700 - 1875 m

nb: Brazilian beans tend to be grown at a lower altitude (500 - 1250 m), which contributes to them being less dense and thus less fruity and bright than other South American coffees.
